Exploring Conflict in Two Wolves

This page helps you explore the different types of conflict found in Two Wolves. We’ll look at Character vs Self, Character vs Others, Character vs Society, and Character vs Nature. You can use examples from the book as we keep reading, or from short summaries provided below.

1️⃣ Character vs Self

Internal conflict happens when a character struggles with their own thoughts, fears, or decisions.

Example: In Chapter 19: The Plan, Ben argues with himself about what’s right and wrong. He feels scared but wants to do what’s right.

What is one example of Ben’s inner struggle from what we’ve read so far?
Write about a time when a character’s feelings stopped them from acting. What would you have done?

2️⃣ Character vs Others

External conflict with other people—arguments, fights, or power struggles.

Example: In Chapter 17: Trapped, Olive argues with Ben and Ray. In Chapter 20: Apricots, Ben and his dad fight over control and truth.

Think of one disagreement Ben has with another character. What causes it?
What does this conflict show us about Ben’s relationship with that person?

3️⃣ Character vs Society

Conflict with rules, laws, or expectations of the world around them.

Example: In Chapter 4: Chase, the police try to stop the car. Ray refuses to follow the law, putting his family at risk.

Why do you think Ray doesn’t stop when the police tell him to?
Do you think Ben agrees with his dad’s choice? Why or why not?

4️⃣ Character vs Nature

When characters face danger or challenges from the natural world—weather, animals, or the wilderness.

Example: In Chapter 10: Culpam Poena Premit Comes, Ben finds nature frightening and hard to survive in. Later, in Chapter 29: Tempest, the wild weather becomes a real threat.

How does Ben react to being in the bush? What does it show about him?
What would you find most difficult about living in a cabin in the middle of nowhere?

Reflection

Which type of conflict do you find most interesting or relatable? Explain why.
