Two Wolves – Reflection Questions

Become a reading detective! Collect evidence from the text and analyse your thinking using this double-entry journal.

How to use this journal

  1. Preview the chapter and note any key questions you have.
  2. Read actively, marking important evidence or quotes with page numbers.
  3. Record the evidence in the left column and explain your thinking on the right.
  4. Look for patterns, surprises, and shifts in character motivations.
  5. Use the wrap-up section to prepare for class discussion or written responses.

Chapters 1–4: Launching the Case

Use these prompts to trace how the sudden getaway sets up the mystery and how Ben begins collecting clues.

Guiding questions

  1. Which details show that April and Ray are keeping secrets from Ben and Olive? Highlight the exact moments.
  2. How does Ben’s passion for detective work help him cope with the trip? Where does it fall short?
  3. What do Olive’s reactions on the drive reveal about her personality and her trust in the adults?
  4. Why does the “two wolves” story matter to Ben so early in the novel?
  5. Which events create the most tension before the family reaches Pop’s cabin?

Chapters 5–8: Survival School

Focus on how the isolation at Pop’s cabin changes the family dynamic and the risks each character faces.

Guiding questions

  3. Which moments show Olive handling the situation differently from Ben?
  4. What clues does Ben collect about the mystery of the duffel bag?
  5. Where do you see Ben choosing between loyalty to family and doing what is right?
